📐 How Projections Are Calculated
▼
📈 Income Projection
Income follows an age-salary parabolic curve peaking at 40–52 (by career field), modified by education multiplier, learning intensity (diminishing returns via log curve), fitness, sleep, networking, stress, and GDP growth.
growth = (base + log(1+learn)×0.028 − screenPen + fitBonus + networkBonus + stressMod) × careerMult × eduMult × ageFactor(age)
💸 Real Income (Fisher Eq)
Real income uses the Fisher equation: nominal income deflated by cumulative inflation. This captures true purchasing power erosion over 20 years using country-specific CPI data.
realIncome = nominalIncome ÷ (1 + inflationRate)^years
🏃 Health (Gompertz Model)
Health decline uses the Gompertz mortality hazard model where deterioration accelerates exponentially with age. Modified by fitness, sleep, screen time, stress, and country HDI (healthcare access proxy).
healthΔ = −(0.0002 × e^(0.05×(age−20)) × 100) + fitnessBonus + sleepBonus − screenPen + stressMod + HDIbonus
🧠 Skill Growth (Log Curve)
Skills compound via logarithmic returns: first hours of learning yield more than later hours. Natural skill decay (0.3%/yr) and screen time drag are subtracted. Education level sets the starting baseline.
skillEffect = log(1 + hours×1.5) / log(1 + 8×1.5) × 4.5 − decay − screenDrag
⚡ Monte Carlo Simulation
200 stochastic iterations with random walks on inflation (±30%), GDP (±50%), career disruptions (5–8% annual probability), health events (2%), and investment return volatility. Produces P10/P50/P90 confidence bands.
P10/P50/P90 = percentile(200 runs, year) | disruption = Bernoulli(p=0.05) × 0.3×income
💰 Net Worth & FIRE
Net worth accumulates via savings (income × savings rate) plus compound investment returns (4/7/10% by risk tolerance). FIRE number = 25× annual expenses (4% safe withdrawal rate). Milestones adjusted for local currency.
netWorth[y] = netWorth[y−1] × (1 + returnRate) + income[y] × savingsRate
⚠️ Limitations
These are illustrative projections, not financial advice. Economic models use simplified assumptions and cannot predict market shocks, career disruptions, health events, or policy changes. Past economic trends may not continue.
